Code of Conduct
  • Integrity
    • Audit firms must act with honesty and truthfulness in all professional and business relationships. No manipulation of financial data or intentional misrepresentation.
  • Objectivity
    • Avoid bias, conflicts of interest, or undue influence. Independent judgment must not be compromised by external pressures (e.g., client relationships).
  • Professional Competence and Due Care
    • Maintain professional knowledge and skills at the required level. Ensure audit work complies with International Standards on Auditing (ISA) and ICAP/SECP guidelines. Adequate training and supervision of staff.
  • Confidentiality
    • Respect confidentiality of client information, even after the audit engagement ends. Do not disclose information to third parties without proper authority unless legally obligated.
  • Professional Behavior
    • Comply with laws and regulations of Pakistan. Avoid any conduct that discredits the profession (e.g., advertising in misleading ways, unprofessional social media behavior, etc.).
  • Independence
    • Independence of mind and independence in appearance are required, especially in statutory audits. ICAP and SECP prescribe rotation of audit partners, cooling-off periods, and limitations on non-audit services to audit clients.
  • Compliance with Laws and Regulations
    • Comply with: Companies Act, 2017 SECP Regulations (e.g., Code of Corporate Governance) Anti-Money Laundering laws Tax laws (for firms involved in tax advisory)
  • Quality Control
    • Firms must implement a system of quality control as per ISQC 1 (International Standard on Quality Control 1) or ISQM 1 (new standard). This includes: Leadership responsibilities Ethical requirements Acceptance and continuance of clients Engagement performance Monitoring
  • Transparency and Reporting
    • Provide honest and fair audit opinions. Disclose any material misstatements or fraud discovered. Report to regulators where necessary (e.g., under whistleblower rules).
  • Accountability
    • Subject to disciplinary action by ICAP and SECP in case of: Professional misconduct Negligence Breach of ethical principles
  • Specific Compliances
    • Listed companies must appoint auditors from the State Bank/SECP approved panel of firms. Firms must rotate audit partners every 5 years for listed entities. SECP conducts inspections and enforces compliance.
