âœ… Assurance Services
These are services where the auditor provides an independent opinion to enhance confidence in financial or non-financial information:
  • - Statutory audits of financial statements
  • - Review engagements (less rigorous than audits but still provide limited assurance)
  • - Compliance audits (e.g., Basel III, anti-money laundering)
  • - Risk assurance and controls testing
  • - Sustainability or ESG assurance, increasingly relevant in modern banking
Assurance services are tightly regulated to preserve auditor independence.
ðŸš« Non-Assurance Services
These are services that do not involve expressing an opinion on financial statements or other subject matter. Examples include:
  • - Tax advisory and planning
  • - Accounting and bookkeeping (though limited for audit clients due to independence concerns)
  • - IT system implementation
  • - Training and consulting
  • - Valuation services
Auditors must be cautious when offering these to audit clients, as they can create conflicts of interest or independence threats.

Some additional services that auditors may provide include:
  • - Forensic audits (investigating fraud or misconduct)
  • - Due diligence for mergers and acquisitions
  • - Regulatory reporting support
  • - Cybersecurity assessments
  • - Internal control consulting
These services are often tailored to the specific needs of financial institutions and must comply with ethical and legal boundaries.
