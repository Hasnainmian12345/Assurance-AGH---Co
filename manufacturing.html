Manufacturing

In the manufacturing sector, auditors play a crucial role not only in financial oversight but also in operational and strategic assurance. Following is a break down of Assurance and Non-Assurance Services, we specifically offer to manufacturers.
✅ Assurance Services in Manufacturing
These services aim to enhance the reliability of information for stakeholders. In manufacturing, they often include:
  1. Financial Statement Audit
    2. - Verifies accuracy of financial records, especially around inventory, cost of goods sold, and capital assets. - Ensures compliance with accounting standards and regulations.
  2. Internal Control Reviews
    3. - Evaluates systems that manage procurement, production, and inventory. - Helps prevent fraud and operational inefficiencies.
  3. Compliance Audits
    4. - Checks adherence to industry regulations (e.g., ISO standards, environmental laws). - Ensures safety and labor law compliance.
  4. Operational Audits
    5. - Assesses efficiency of manufacturing processes. - Identifies waste, bottlenecks, and areas for improvement.
  5. Sustainability Assurance
    6. - Reviews ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) reporting. - Validates claims about energy use, emissions, and ethical sourcing.
❎ Non-Assurance Services in Manufacturing
These services do not involve an independent opinion but still add value. Examples include:
  1. Tax Advisory
    2. - Helps optimize tax strategies related to manufacturing operations and capital investments.
  2. Consulting Services
    3. - Advises on ERP implementation, supply chain optimization, and lean manufacturing techniques.
  3. Training & Capacity Building
    4. - Offers workshops on internal controls, risk management, and compliance.
  4. Due Diligence for M&A
    5. - Supports mergers or acquisitions by analyzing financial and operational data.
  5. Forensic Investigations
    6. - Assists in uncovering fraud or misconduct within manufacturing operations.
⚠️ Note: We maintain independence when offering non-assurance services to audit clients. Regulatory frameworks restrict certain services to avoid conflicts of interest.
